\chapter{Experiments and Results}\label{chap:experiments}

This chapter establishes the empirical framework used to evaluate the capabilities of noise-resilient Automated Algorithm Design (AAD) loops and delivers a comprehensive performance analysis of the evolved heuristics. To systematically assess the influence of environmental stochasticity on language model code generation, we outline a series of highly controlled optimization tournaments matching our noise-evolved agents against clean-evolved control models. The evaluation trajectory moves from tracking generation-by-generation progression inside the evolutionary loop to validating the final champion codes across previously unseen landscapes, testing both generalizability limits and structural scalability boundaries. Through rigorous statistical evaluation of global minima convergence rates paired with a white-box qualitative autopsy of the final program source files, this chapter provides a direct, quantifiable baseline indicating under which precise conditions an LLM can independently formulate robust metaheuristics capable of cutting through deceptive noise floors.
