\chapter*{Proposal Summary}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Proposal Summary}

Continuous black-box optimization forms a critical backbone for modern parameter extraction and complex engineering simulations, yet traditional human-engineered metaheuristics fundamentally degrade when traversing non-deterministic environments. In real-world applications, empirical evaluations are frequently corrupted by stochastic noise profiles which obscure underlying landscapes, introducing deceptive local optima and statistical anomalies that trap conventional gradient-free search rules. Historically, mitigating these challenges required years of laborious manual derivation by human experts to append specialized filtering or step-size adaptation rules—an engineering bottleneck that is highly inefficient due to the vast topological diversity across different problem classes. To bypass this manual limitation, this thesis introduces a systematic research framework utilizing Automated Algorithm Design (AAD) via Large Language Models (LLMs) to transition from static parameter tuning to the autonomous synthesis of executable code structures. By grounding our pipeline in the Large Language Model Evolutionary Algorithm (LLaMEA) framework, this project establishes a closed-loop evolutionary system where a generative model is subjected to controlled non-deterministic environment pressures. By strategically exposing the generative model to specialized noise distributions during code assessment, we force the LLM to reflexively adapt programmatic logic and discover independent uncertainty-handling mechanisms. This research will culminate in a rigorous empirical benchmark testing the cross-environment generalizability of noise-specialized heuristics against equivalents evolved under clean, deterministic conditions, thereby providing a quantifiable indicator of an LLM's capacity for autonomous scientific and engineering discovery under uncertainty.
